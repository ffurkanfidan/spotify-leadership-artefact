Leadership Theories

"Heroes" - David Bowie

Transformational Leadership (Burns, 1978)

Concept:

Transformational leadership: David Bowie encourages positive change and development in this song. The song's emotional tone, along with the lyrics "we can be heroes, just for one day," motivates the listener.

"Human" - Rag'n'Bone Man

Authentic Leadership (George, 2003)

Concept:

Authentic Leadership: Rag'n'Bone Man, in this song, says "I'm only human," explaining that humans make mistakes and should be judged accordingly. A good leader can make mistakes themselves and accept those they lead for their mistakes. This song emphasizes self-awareness, transparency, and compassion.

"Under Pressure" - Queen & David Bowie

Adaptive Leadership (Heifetz, 1994)

Concept:

Adaptive Leadership represents coping with challenges at different levels, leading under pressure and uncertainty. It teaches us that pressure is normal, but we must give ourselves another chance and not give up.

"Lean on Me" - Bill Withers

Servant Leadership (Greenleaf, 1970)

Concept:

Servant leadership is based on the philosophy that you must serve first, then expect service. The song expects a leader to put themselves in someone else's shoes. The song emphasizes the importance of supporting each other and that a leader will always provide this.

"Team" - Lorde

Distributed Leadership (Spillane, 2006)

Concept:

Distributed leadership emphasizes that leadership resides in the team, not in one person. Lorde's lyric "We're on each other's team" in the song emphasizes the importance of teamwork and supporting each other rather than being a stumbling block.

Motivation & Management

"Lose Yourself" - Eminem

Motivation (Content Theory, Purpose) (Pink, 2009)

Concept:

In this song, Eminem emphasizes the importance of never wasting an opportunity, working hard, and never giving up. He reminds us that despite all the challenges, there is always a way out. He advises seizing the moment and not dwelling on the past.

"Viva la Vida" - Coldplay

Ethics & Responsible Management

Concept:

In this song, Coldplay describes themselves as a leader in decline. They explain that things may not always go well, and that sometimes things can go wrong. Therefore, a leader should not assume they will be a leader forever and should conduct their actions within a specific ethical framework. The fact that the album cover is one of the most important and famous images of the French Revolution speaks to the song's belief in the power of change.

"Thinking Out Loud" - Ed Sheeran

Six 'Thinking Hats' (De Bono, 1985)

Concept:

De Bono's Six Hat Thinking Technique aims to solve problems through different ways of thinking. This song, too, evokes a problem-solving style dominated by emotional intelligence and intuition, dressed in a red hat. This song highlights the emotional side of people and contributes to finding solutions that cannot be achieved through logic alone.

"Whatever It Takes" - Imagine Dragons

Strategic Planning & Vision Setting

Concept:

The song discusses the importance of strategic planning and the need for determination to achieve long-term goals. The phrase "Whatever It Takes," which also serves as the song's title and appears in the song itself, emphasizes the determination, resilience, and willpower required to achieve a set goal.

"Heal the World" - Michael Jackson

Design Thinking & Empathy (Kelley, 2005)

Concept:

The cornerstone of Design Thinking is empathy. This song advises understanding the needs of others, setting out to help others with compassion, and taking responsibility for a better world. This promotes being people-centered and caring about the problems faced by others.
